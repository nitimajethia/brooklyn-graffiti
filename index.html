Brooklyn's graffiti problem, decoded

Brooklyn, a borough rooted in values of culture and expression — is bearing the brunt of a clandestine art movement.
Here is a comprehensive view.

By Niti Majethia

December 15, 2023

“Graffiti inherently started as a creative outlet for kids who couldn’t afford to go to camp or do things like that,” said Jonathan Cohen aka Meres Ones, the Founder and Curator of 5 Pointz, the art mural space in Queens that was a go-to for those who wanted to create, before it was demolished in 2014. He highlighted that kids need to find a way to entertain themselves. It is also how someone feels noticed, in a city full of millions of people. “It’s a way of saying — I was here,” he said.
Cohen talked about how he understands the frustration of property owners, but often, graffiti is also done to beautify the neighbourhoods. “If graffiti has increased, we should find a medium ground instead of trying to annihilate it,” he explained, emphasizing that 5 Pointz, while being a creative outlet in the neighbourhood, also became a tourist attraction that benefitted all the businesses around. However, not all New Yorkers agree with Cohen's sentiment of graffiti beautifying neighbourhoods.
The NYC 311 system shows that Brooklyn’s District 1 had the maximum number of requests for graffiti removal. In this story, we decode Brooklyn's graffiti complaints through its Community Districts.
